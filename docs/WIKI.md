## Features

### 🗺️ The Learning Map (Home)
The home screen shows your child's grade as a map of **strands** (topics) full of **skill
cards**. Cards show stars earned and a ✓ when mastered; locked cards (🔒) unlock as
prerequisites are mastered. Up top: a **Keep going** card (jump back into the last skill), a
**Review time** card when skills are due, and a **Daily goal** bar.

### 📘 Lessons
Tapping a new skill starts a short, guided lesson with the mascot: a hook, the big idea, 2–4
teaching steps (often with a picture), and the **Common Core standard** it covers. Then it
flows into practice.

### ✏️ Practice (the core loop)
Infinite, freshly generated problems. For each one your child can:
- **💡 Hint** — a 3-step ladder of nudges (orientation → strategy → almost-there).
- **📖 Show me how** — the full **step-by-step worked solution**, revealed one step at a time,
  with the picture alongside.
Right answers celebrate (XP, coins, confetti); wrong answers get a kind "not yet" and help —
never "wrong!". After 3 tries the solution is shown and a fresh similar problem appears.
Clearing the skill's target count **masters** it (1–3 stars based on first-try accuracy).

### 🎚️ Adaptive difficulty
Within a session, difficulty ramps up after a couple of correct answers and eases off after a
couple of misses — keeping your child in the "just right" zone.

### 🧠 Spaced review
Mastery isn't "done and forgotten." Mastered skills come back for a quick refresh on a
lengthening schedule (3 → 7 → 16 → 35 → 70 days). This **retrieval practice** is one of the
most evidence-backed ways to make learning stick.

### 🧭 Placement quiz
At first run (optional) a quick check finds the right starting grade, so your child isn't bored
or overwhelmed.

### 🖼️ Pictures that teach (manipulatives)
Concepts are shown, not just told: number lines, base-ten blocks, multiplication arrays,
fraction bars & circles, equal groups, area grids, clocks, money, percent bars, ratio tape
diagrams, 3-D volume boxes, and data dot plots — all drawn live.

### 🏆 Rewards (Treasures)
- **XP & Levels** — earned by practicing; level-ups feel great.
- **Coins** — earned and spent in the **Shop** (hats, glasses, capes, crowns, themes,
  backgrounds) and on **Pets** (collectible companions; the first is free).
- **Badges** — 36 milestones from "First Steps" to mastering a whole strand.
- **Daily goal & Streaks** — a gentle daily target and a positively framed day streak with a
  built-in grace day (no guilt, no harsh resets).

### 🐾 Pet Home (care)
Your pet lives in a cozy home. **Pat** it (free), **Play** (free — and it sometimes finds a
coin), or **Feed** it a treat. A **happiness meter** and a **fullness meter** drift down gently
over time and refill when you visit and interact — it's designed to be delightful, never
guilt-inducing (the pet is never neglected-sad). **Treats** are bought with coins in the home's
little treat shop, giving coins a warm purpose. Adventuring also makes your pet happier.

### ⚔️ Pet Quest (story adventure + tutor mode)
A 3-chapter, 15-scene story where **your pet is the hero** — the Whispering Woods, the Sparkle
Caves, and the Cloud Castle. Each scene tells a bit of story and then blocks the path with a
**4th-grade math challenge**. Crucially, new concepts are **taught before they're tested**
(*tutor mode*): the tutor shows the big idea and a **fully worked example** step-by-step, asks
your child to **explain why it works** to their pet (self-explanation), and *then* hands over a
problem to try — with the hint ladder and "show me how" always available. Clearing a scene
advances the story and rewards coins, treats, and pet happiness; finishing a chapter unlocks
the next. (This is the most pedagogically important mode — it's built on the research in
`docs/research/tutor-design.md` and `docs/research/learning-science.md`.)

### 🦊 Foxy, the guide
A friendly fox who teaches, hints, and cheers — with several expressions and optional
read-aloud.

### 👨‍👩‍👧 Grown-ups Corner (Parent dashboard)
Level, streak, coins, badges, problems solved, accuracy, lessons, skills mastered; **mastery
by topic**; a **Curriculum & trust** panel (Common Core alignment, privacy, accessibility); a
**printable curriculum map**; the app version + "What's New"; and settings.

### ⚙️ Settings & accessibility
Sound, **read-aloud (TTS)**, **calm mode** (reduced motion), **easy-reading font**, starting
grade, name, and reset. Plus visible focus, pinch-zoom, color-blind-safe answer states, and
keyboard navigation.

### 🔄 Auto-updates
Installed as an app, MathQuest checks for new versions and offers a one-tap **Update now**;
a **"What's New"** sheet summarizes each release.

## Glossary

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Skill** | The smallest learnable unit (e.g., "Multiply 2-digit by 1-digit"). 60 in total. |
| **Strand** | A topic group (Place Value, Multiplication, Fractions, …). |
| **Mastery** | Clearing a skill's target number of problems; earns 1–3 stars. |
| **Problem type** | An engine recipe that generates a problem + solution + hints (25 of them). |
| **Manipulative** | A visual math model (fraction bar, number line, array, …). |
| **Spaced review** | Revisiting mastered skills on a widening schedule to aid memory. |
| **Adaptive difficulty** | Automatic tuning of problem hardness within a session. |
| **Standard (CCSS)** | A Common Core State Standard code (e.g., `4.NF.B.3`) each skill aligns to. |
| **PWA** | Progressive Web App — installable, offline-capable web app. |

## Parent FAQ

**Is it free? Are there ads?**
Yes, free. No ads, ever.

**Does it need the internet?**
Only to load the first time (or to install). After that it works **fully offline**.

**What data do you collect?**
None leaves the device. Progress is stored locally in the browser. No accounts, no tracking.
See [PRIVACY.md](PRIVACY.md).

**Which grades / standards does it cover?**
Grades 3–6, aligned to the Common Core. See the [Curriculum Map](CURRICULUM.md) (also
printable from the parent dashboard).

**My child is ahead/behind their grade — can I change it?**
Yes. Use the grade tabs on the home screen, the placement quiz, or the starting-grade setting
in the Grown-ups Corner.

**How do I install it on a tablet/phone?**
Open the site and choose "Add to Home Screen." It then launches like a normal app and works
offline.

**Will my child just chase rewards instead of learning?**
The design deliberately keeps rewards supportive, not the point: micro-celebrations, a daily
goal, grace-day streaks, and surprise bonuses — no loot boxes, no pay-to-win, no guilt.

**How does it handle wrong answers?**
Encouragingly. First miss: "not quite — try a different way?" Then hints, then a full
worked solution, then a fresh similar problem. Growth-mindset language throughout.

**How do updates work?**
The app updates itself: when a new version is available it offers a one-tap update, and shows
a "What's New" summary.

**Is it accessible?**
Yes — keyboard navigation, screen-reader labels, color-blind-safe states, a dyslexia-friendly
font, calm (reduced-motion) mode, and read-aloud.
